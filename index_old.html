What I'm reading these days

I'm reading this book recently: The Phoenix Project. It talks about DevOps management and is both informative and fun to read. I highly recommend it!

Other books I'm reading or have read recently and highly recommend:

  • How Computers Work - This book breaks down complex concepts such as microprocessors, transistors and digital signal processing into super clear and easy-to-understand explanations, accompanied by plenty of illustrative images. It's perfect for beginners like myself who want to grasp the nuts and bolts of how computer hardware and software function.
  • The Body - Written by Bill Bryson, this book offers a fascinating exploration of the human body. Bryson explains the complexities of human anatomy and physiology in an engaging and accessible manner, making it a must-read for anyone interested in understanding how our bodies work.
  • The Art of Doing Science and Engineering: Learning to Learn - Richard Hamming (Turing Award winner)'s classic book. Richard himself is a history book; his career and experiences offer a lot of incrediblely deep insights into how computers and AI have evolved to this date since the beginning.
